一, On Scalar-Tensor Theoies and its Origin

Arxiv 1806.02811 Spatially Covariant Gravity with Velocity of the lapse function
Summary & Reference

  1. During the past dacade, tremendous progress have been made in extending the scope of sclar-tensor theories. The representitive achievements along this line are the k-essence—the most general theory for covariant scalar fields involing first derivatives in Lagrangian. [hep-th/9904075], [astro-ph/9912463]. And the Horndeski theory—the most general covariant scalar-tensor theory involving up to second derivatives both in the Lagrangian and in the equations of motion. [1103.3260], [1105.5723]. And other theory beyond Horndeski in which higher derivatives were introduced in the Lagrangian while keeping the theories healthy by imposing degeneracy. [1404.4695], [1408.1952], [1510.06930], [1512.06820], [1601.04658], [1602.03119], [1602.08398], [1603.01269], [1603.09355], [1604.08638], [1608.08135], [1610.07980], [1703.01623].

An alternative and yet more powerful approach to the scalar-tensor theories is to construct theories that do not respect the full symmetry of GR, i.e., the spacetime diffeomorphism. In fact, the “scalar” is not necessarily a covariant scalar field, but is essentially an effective scalar-type physical degree of freedom. It is well-known that for theories with gauge redundancies, new degrees of freedom may arise by reducing the gauge symmetries. Theories along this line can be traced back to the effective field theory (EFT) of inflation. [hep-th/0606090], [0709.0293], 以及 Horava gravity [0901.3775], [0909.3525]. The EFT can be applied to dark matter: [0811.0827], [1210.0201], [1211.7054], [1304.4840], [1304.6712], [1411.3712], [1504.05481], [1509.02191]. These theories are metric theories that respect only spatial symmetry instead of the full spacetime symmetry. Therefore, we may refer to such kinds of theories as subsets of “spatially covariant gravity”.

Recent attempts trying to relate these two approaches have brought us new insights into the scalar-tensor theories. When fixing the gauge by choosing the scalar field as the time coordinate, which is dubbed as the “unitary gauge” in the literature), the Horndeski Lagrangian can be recast in a form similar to that of the EFT of inflation/dark energy [1304.4840].
