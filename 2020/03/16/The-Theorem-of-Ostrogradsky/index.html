Nature of the Instability

Ostrogradsky’s result implies that the Hamiltonian of a nondegenerate higher order derivatives theory is unbounded below, and also above. This section discusses the manner in which the instabilities manifests, and what it implies for fundamental physics. Six short subsections make the point:

  1. The Ostrogradskian instability drives the canonical variables to a special time dependence, not a speical numerical value.
  2. The same Ostrogradiskian variables carries both positive and negative energy creation and annihilation operaters.
  3. the empty state of a system which suffers from O’s instability can decay into a collection of postive and negative energy excitations.
  4. If a system suffers from O’s instability is a contiuum field theory, the vast entropy of infinite 3-monmentum will make the decay instantaneous.
  5. For interacting system which suffers from O’s instability, degrees of freedom with large 3-monmentum do not decouple from low energy physics.
  6. The imposition of a sigle, global constrain on energy functional does not ameliorate the O’s instability.

kinetic Instability

Physicists are familiar with instabilities of the potential energy. In this case energy is released when dynamical variables approach some speical valuse: potential energy depends only upon the value of $x$. The O’s instability is instead a problem with kinetic energy, and it manifests by the dynamical variables developing a speical time dependece: the kinetic energy depends both on $\ddot{x}$ and $x^{(3)}$, which can only be calculated once the time dependece is solved.

Double Duty for Dynamical Variables.

Physicists are used to resolving linearized dynamical variables into creation and annihilation operators. In the case of a harmonic oscillator, this is done by using Euler relation to identify a lowering operator proportional to $e^{-i\omega t}$, and a raising operator proportional to $e^{i\omega t}$.

The usual rule is that one dynamical variables harbors either zeor or only one set of creation and annihilation operators at linearized order. However, the variables solved by Hamiltonian with hiher times derivatives carries/ possesses/ contains/ harbors both positive and negative energy ladder operators. The local interaction which evolve the variables necessarily couple the two sectors.

The Vacuum Can Decay

Now consider a interacting, contiuum field theory that possesses O’s instability.

Entropy Drives Vacuum Decay

Recall the reason that the excited states of an atom decay in nature. It’s certainly not to reduce the total energy in the system—including the anomic system and the electromagnetic field—but instead/ rather redistribute the constant total energy into the largest possible classes of states and to obtain the largest possible entropy of the system. The is only one way for the atom not to decay, compared with an infinite way it can decay and emit one or more recoil phonons. Note also that the explicit computations of the decay time employ vacuum fluctruations of electromagnetic field to provide the necessary pertubation.

The decay of a continumm, interacting and nondegenerate field theory can involve particles of any energy, as long as the total sums to zero. The volume of the phase space is so large that it can cause/ make the decay instantaneous. The only way people can computet he finite decay rate for particles with kinetic instability is by cutting off the phase space at some specific point, in which case the decay rate is dominated by the cutoff. Such cutoff might make sense if the kinetic instability appears in some nonlocal effective field theory, but it has no place in fundamental physics.

Note that decay does not happen just once. It is even more entripicly favoured for there to be two decay, and better yet for more. Whether or not it has proper mathematic representations, it certainly does not describe the real world us human experience in which all particles have postive energy and empty state remains empty.

Note that this conclusion only follows if the higher derivative theory possesses both interaction and continuum particles. The point particle oscillator does not have interactions, so its negative energy is unobservable. The feature that drives explosive vacuum decay is the vast entropy of phyase space. Without that it remains an open question whether or not there is anything wrong with higher derivative theory.
